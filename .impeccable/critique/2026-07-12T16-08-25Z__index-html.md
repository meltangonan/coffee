## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not slop. A barista fluent in category tools would trust this — freshness is a real derived state, the extraction visualizers encode genuine targets, and the Stats voice is disciplined and observational. None of the shared absolute bans meaningfully fire. Two conscious tells: Playfair (a display serif) renders numeric *data* (stat counts, ratios, seconds) — defensible "notebook" editorial choice but on the line; and uppercase `.stats-card-label` mini-titles brush the per-section-eyebrow pattern while functioning as card titles.

**Deterministic scan (`detect.mjs`, exit 2, 104 findings):**
- `design-system-font-size` — 93 advisory hits: font sizes off the DESIGN.md type ramp. Mostly conventional sizes (16/15/14/11px) in a single-file app that predates a strict ramp — bulk advisory noise, not per-line defects, but a real signal the type scale isn't enforced.
- `design-system-color` — 5 hits (L156–159): `rgba(0,0,0,…)` overlay tints and an off-token destructive red `#a04038`. Borderline — worth a token decision.
- `design-system-radius` — 5 hits (L163, 366, 391, 505, 563): radii off the 6/10/14/20 scale.
- `layout-transition` — 1 warning (L278): `transition: width` on `.shot-visualizer-fill`. Low impact (thin progress fill), likely intentional.

**Visual overlays (injection succeeded):** overlay reported `1 anti-pattern` carrying 2 findings, identical across all four tabs: `cream-palette` (the `#F5F0E8` body bg) and `layout-transition` (L278). The `cream-palette` hit is a **false positive** — it's the documented, deliberate brand background, not the AI-default cream trap.

## Overall Impression

This is a genuinely designed product, not a generated one — the strongest moments (delete confirmation, trend charts, observational stats copy) read as expert-tool craft. The biggest single opportunity is **accessibility contrast**: the muted-brown-on-cream palette that gives the app its warmth currently fails WCAG AA across secondary text, every link, and most state badges — and PRODUCT.md explicitly commits to AA. Fixing contrast protects the brand's warmth while making the app readable.

## What's Working

1. **Delete-bean confirmation** — names the bean, states the exact shot count ("12 shots"), and "This cannot be undone." Honest, specific, non-generic; textbook for an irreversible action.
2. **Trends charts** — real y-axes with tick labels, target bands, rotated axis titles, amber line over dark marks. The most "expert tool" moment in the app; trustworthy, category-fluent data viz.
3. **Stats voice & usefulness** — decision-useful (sweet-spot quartiles, extraction-zone distribution) and rigorously observational ("71% of your 34 shots in the standard range"). Faithfully executes the anti-motivational brand stance.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Pervasive contrast failures (WCAG AA).** Measured against real backgrounds: `--text-muted #9B8B7E` = 2.9:1 on cream / 3.29:1 on white (used for dates, meta, placeholders, stepper defaults, everywhere); `--amber-dark #B8864E` as text = 2.82:1 on cream (used for *every* link: "Fill from previous," "Manage," datepicker "Today"/"Clear"); tinted badges Resting 2.24, At Peak 2.38, Okay 2.52, Past Peak 3.46 — only blue "Great" (5.17) passes.
- *Why it matters:* secondary info and all links are hard to read; accessibility-dependent users are locked out; violates PRODUCT.md's stated AA baseline.
- *Fix:* darken `--text-muted` toward ~`#6E5D50` (≥4.5 on cream & white), use `--espresso`/darker token for link text, darken badge text tokens. Keep the hues; deepen them.
- *Command:* `/impeccable audit` (verify) then `/impeccable colorize` or `/impeccable polish` (fix tokens).

**[P1] Placeholder-as-default ambiguity (shot steppers).** Applied defaults (grind 3, dose 18, yield 34, time 24) render in muted placeholder grey — indistinguishable from "empty," yet they're what gets saved. A first-timer can't tell whether a value is set.
- *Why it matters:* undermines the core-loop trust; user can't glance-confirm the recipe at the machine.
- *Fix:* when a default is applied, render it as a real filled value in primary text (optional "from your last shot" helper).
- *Command:* `/impeccable clarify` or `/impeccable layout`.

**[P2] Core-loop friction.** "Save Shot" sits below the fold on a 375px screen, beneath the visualizer, date, and notes — and the *optional*, record-only Portafilter field is placed *first*, above Grind. The 10-second one-handed promise is undercut.
- *Why it matters:* the most-repeated action fights the primary design principle.
- *Fix:* sticky Save bar; lead with the core recipe; demote portafilter/date/notes.
- *Command:* `/impeccable layout`.

**[P2] Consistency & color-semantics collisions.** Home labels the metric "Current streak" (motivational, off-voice) while Stats calls the identical number "Current run · brew days" (on-voice). Separately, the Calendar's per-bean identity ribbons reuse the exact green/terracotta/blue of the freshness stages shown in the legend directly below.
- *Why it matters:* the "streak" wording is the first step onto the motivational path the brand forbids; the color reuse invites misreading ribbon hue as freshness state.
- *Fix:* unify wording to "run/brew days" on Home; give bean ribbons a distinct hue family or a direct label.
- *Command:* `/impeccable clarify` (wording) + `/impeccable colorize` (ribbon hues).

**[P3] Destructive-action guarding.** Shot swipe-delete is immediate and irreversible (unlike bean delete); and in the delete dialog the red "Delete Bean" is the visually dominant top button.
- *Fix:* undo toast for shot delete; de-emphasize the destructive button relative to Cancel.
- *Command:* `/impeccable harden`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (one-handed, at the machine):** must thumb-scroll to reach Save on the primary action; the bean picker combobox sits at the top of the screen (hard thumb reach); muted default values make the recipe hard to glance-confirm; swipe-delete-with-no-undo is a real accidental-loss risk.

**Jordan (first-timer):** can't tell if stepper "3" is their grind or a suggestion; sees "Bad" + green "Well-extracted" with no explanation; the Ratio/Time/Flow bars, green target bands, and "1.4 g/s" arrive with no legend; the duplicate-bean icon in the detail header is unlabeled.

**Sam (contrast / keyboard / screen reader):** fails 4.5:1 on muted text, all links, and freshness/quality badges (primary blocker). Custom comboboxes mark `role="combobox"`/`listbox` but options are `div[role=option]` toggled by opacity with no arrow-key roving focus or `aria-activedescendant`; opening the trigger doesn't move focus into the list. Star rating uses `<span>` click targets, not buttons — keyboard operability unclear. Icon-only buttons do carry `aria-label` (good). `:focus-visible` coverage is inconsistent.

## Minor Observations

- Playfair on stat numerals is a borderline "display font in data" call — defensible as brand, but conscious.
- Monthly-recap axis "J A S O N D J F M A M J" is ambiguous (repeated letters, no year).
- The same three metrics (ratio/time/flow) appear three ways on one bean-detail scroll — cohesive but repetitive.
- 93 font sizes drift off the DESIGN.md type ramp; 5 radii off the 6/10/14/20 scale — the token system exists but isn't enforced.
- The `feTurbulence` paper-grain background is subtle and appropriate.

## Questions to Consider

1. If the promise is a 10-second one-handed log at a hot machine, why does Save live below the fold beneath *optional* metadata — is this a "quick log" or a "journal entry," and can one form honestly be both?
2. The product quietly grades nearly every shot "Well-extracted," even when the user says it was bad. Whose judgment is it deferring to — the palate or the extraction math — and what happens to trust when they disagree?
3. Home celebrates a "Current streak" while the brand forbids streaks and Stats calls the same number a "run." Which is the real product — a calm notebook or a habit tracker?
