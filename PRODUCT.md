# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Home baristas, broadly — anyone pulling espresso at home who rotates through beans and forgets what worked last time. Their context is standing at the machine: one hand free, a few seconds of patience, often on a phone. They want a quick reference for what dialed in, not a café POS system or pro roasting software. The app is public and account-free; a first-time visitor and a daily regular use the same surface.

## Product Purpose

Coffee Journal tracks beans, logs shots (grind, dose, yield, time, taste), and shows when each coffee is inside its freshness window (resting / at peak / past peak). It exists so the next shot starts from the last good one instead of from memory. Success is a shot logged in seconds and a confident answer to "what settings did I use on this bean?"

## Positioning

The fastest way to log a shot and recall what worked, while standing at the espresso machine. Every screen should reinforce speed-to-log and speed-to-recall.

## Brand Personality

Warm, calm, precise. Café warmth in the visuals, an unhurried tone in the copy, and exact numbers where they matter. Stats and insights are observational, never motivational — facts about the user, no records to beat, no nudges, no exclamation marks.

## Anti-references

- Fitness-app gamification: streaks-as-pressure, badges, confetti, motivational nudges.
- Café POS / pro software (Cropster-style): dense inventory tooling, data-entry drudgery.
- Generic SaaS dashboard: cool grays, KPI hero cards, sparklines everywhere.
- Instagram-café twee: precious latte-art aesthetic, script fonts, decoration over utility.

## Design Principles

1. **One hand, ten seconds.** The core loop (pick bean → log shot) must survive a distracted user at a hot machine. Anything that adds taps to that path needs to earn them.
2. **Recall over analytics.** Data shown answers "what should I do with this shot?" before it answers "how am I trending?" Decision-useful beats fun-fact.
3. **Observational voice.** The app describes; it never cheers, nags, or scores the user.
4. **Polish surfaces, never remove substance.** Refinement passes may tighten visuals but must not strip information the user relies on at the machine.
5. **Warmth without costume.** The café character lives in type, color, and tone — not in decorative flourishes that slow the tool down.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, touch targets ≥44px, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, and color never the only signal (freshness states pair color with labels).
